(Screenshot/UNTV) Short Url https://arab.news/nvat8 Updated 01 July 2026 20:19 ARAB NEWS July 01, 2026 20:07 Follow The meeting is expected to take place on Thursday, with Bahrain’s Foreign Minister arriving in New York on Wednesday to participate in the session NEW YORK: Bahrain on Wednesday requested an emergency meeting of the UN Security Council following Iran’s recent attack on the kingdom, Arab News has learned. The meeting is expected to take place on Thursday, with Bahrain’s Foreign Minister Abdullatif Bin Rashid Al-Zayani arriving in New York on Wednesday to participate in the session. The request comes amid heightened tensions in the Gulf despite a US-Iran agreement signed on June 17 aimed at ending military hostilities. Since then, Iran has targeted a commercial vessel it said had deviated from its approved route through the Strait of Hormuz, while US Central Command said it struck 10 Iranian military targets over the weekend. Tehran subsequently launched attacks on US military bases in Kuwait and Bahrain, prompting condemnation from Gulf Cooperation Council countries.

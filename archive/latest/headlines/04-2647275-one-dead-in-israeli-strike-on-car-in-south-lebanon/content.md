BEIRUT: An Israeli strike in the south of Lebanon on Monday killed one person, state media said, the first reported deadly strike since the announcement of a deal to end the Middle East war on all fronts. "Enemy drone aircraft targeted a car at the Kfar Tibnit roundabout, leading to the death of its driver," the National News Agency (NNA) said, referring to a village near the city of Nabatieh. Earlier, Lebanon's army urged displaced residents to delay their return to southern border villages, hours after the agreement was announced between Washington and Tehran to end the Middle East war, including in Lebanon. Since early Monday, AFP correspondents reported a cautious return of residents to several south Lebanon areas where Israeli forces are not present.
